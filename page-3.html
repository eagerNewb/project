Get involved with the Ickle Pickless

The simple fact is that more and more babies require specialised care when they are born than ever before.

This is down to several factors including advances in treatment and technology leading to better care and improved survival for babies born extremely prematurely (as early as 22 or 23 weeks). There are an increased number of infants successfully managed with complex congenital surgical problems, and increased numbers of preterm multiple births from the success of fertility treatments.

In 1975 the survival rate for a premature baby was less than 50%, today that figure is over 90%.  Treatment has become so successful that babies who would have been unlikely to survive just ten years ago can survive and thrive - at St. George’s Hospital in South London, the smallest baby born this year was just 430g when born at 22 weeks - with babies that tiny, the skin is practically translucent and can be bruised by the slightest touch.

The increasing incidence of premature birth and the improving survival rates mean incredible strains are being placed on the budgets, infrastructure and equipment being used to treat them, especially as technological advances accelerate research opportunities and new treatments.

The pace of change in Neonatal care will never be this slow again.

Become an ambassador

For some people, the call to riase funds or make a difference to Neonatal care is very strong.  If running a marathon is not for you, then maybe you should consider becoming an Ickle Pickles Ambassador.

Our Ambassadors own the relationship with a specific Neonatal Unit on behalf of the charity and typically do 3 things.

1 - Run a coffee / social morning once per month for parents and children who have been treated at the Neonatal Unit - the support and social interaction with other peopel who share the Neonatal experience can be very comforting and rewarding.

2 - Build a relationship with the clinical and nursing staff to help determine what equipment is most urgently needed

3 - Manage a noticeboard within the Neonatal unit that contains informaiton about the charity and fundraising events and opportunities.

If this sounds like something you'd like to explore, please get in touch today.

How to set up online fundraising

We are registered with BT MyDonate & Just Giving websites. The great advantage is that these organisations collect the 25% Gift Aid on your donations and sponsorship on our behalf.  They do take a small fee, however this is deducted from the Gift Aid so we can guarantee that over 100% of your donation or monies raised will go towards vital Neonatal equipment.

We are not registered with Virgin Giving

Incubators

Incubators regulate a baby’s environment providing a sterile and protective environment that simulates conditions in the womb and facilitates effective low-risk clinical treatment

SIPAP machines

SIPAP machines provides constant air pressure support within a babies vulnerable lungseasing breathing and reduces the effort for these babies to simply breathe
